# BAA Operations Policy

Status: draft for HIPAA readiness.

Owner: Privacy/Legal Owner.

## Purpose

Ensure PHI workloads are accepted only after appropriate Business Associate Agreement review and operational gating.

## Policy

- BAA must be signed before PHI production traffic.
- BAA must identify the covered entity/business associate customer, permitted uses, safeguards, subcontractor obligations, reporting obligations, HHS access, termination, and return/destroy terms.
- Customer must approve route restrictions before PHI traffic.
- BAA status must be recorded in the customer workspace record or compliance tracker.
- BAA exceptions require counsel approval.

## Execution Checklist

1. Confirm customer legal name and HIPAA role.
2. Confirm permitted use case.
3. Confirm PHI data types.
4. Review and sign BAA.
5. Review subprocessors and downstream model providers.
6. Configure route restrictions.
7. Confirm content export disabled or separately approved.
8. Confirm incident contacts.
9. Verify trust page and security docs delivered.
10. Record go-live approval.

## Evidence

- Signed BAA.
- Workspace compliance status.
- Route approval.
- Subprocessor approval.
- Customer go-live approval.
